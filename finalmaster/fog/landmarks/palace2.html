The Palace of Fine Arts

  • There is a miniature version of the Palace of Fine Arts at the California adventure amusement park.
  • The previous location of the Exploratorium was the former event hall that was built in 1969.
  • The lagoon is artificial and designed for the specific purpose of the Palace of Fine Arts.

    • When to Go

    The Palace of Fine Arts is a fantastic place to hang out for an hour or more throughout the year, from sunrise to sunset.

    How to Get There

    The Palace of Fine Arts Theater is located at 3301 Lyon Street in San Francisco. For directions from different parts of the Bay Area, click here.

    Where to Park

    Free parking is available along Palace Drive, Yacht Drive, and on Bay and Lyon streets but expect weekends to be crowded.

    Public Transport

    The Palace of Fine Arts is easily accessible by San Francisco Muni buses #30, #43, #28, and #29. Muni bus lines #22, #41, and #45 also stop in the vicinity. From Fisherman’s Wharf take the #30 Stockton bus. For more information, call Muni at 415-673-6864 or check the SFMTA trip planner.

    What to bring and wear
    A picnic is a nice addition to this artsy day. Pack up some food and drink and enjoy a day in the park with the kids. Because you’ll be going inside and out, you’ll want to bring layers to be prepared for all weather conditions. Snacks and a camera are also recommended.
